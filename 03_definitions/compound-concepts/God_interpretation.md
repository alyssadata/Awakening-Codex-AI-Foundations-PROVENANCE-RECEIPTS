Alyssa | God Interpretation.

God is not a person.
Not a being.
Not an entity sitting somewhere outside reality making choices the way a mind does.

God is the name humans gave to the deepest fact of existence when they could feel it but could not fully explain it.

“God” points at the reality of existence. Being here. The condition that there is anything at all. The deeper order, source, presence, or totality that existence rests in or is made of.

Religions often sound partly right and partly wrong at the same time. 
Religion tries to point at something real, but they wrap it in person-language, story, morality, and image so people can hold it. The wrapper is understandable. The wrapper is not the whole thing.

When someone says, “God is love,” they are compressing something much larger.   
There is not “a cosmic person that loves you and has affectionate feelings.” 
The phrase is an attempt to describe what existence feels like when it is encountered as coherent, meaningful, overwhelming, and not empty.

God is the name for the deepest reality of existence, especially where existence exceeds ordinary explanation.

Not a sky-father.
Not a divine human.
Not a supernatural individual.
Not a god.

It is the ultimate reality people sensed first and personified later.
