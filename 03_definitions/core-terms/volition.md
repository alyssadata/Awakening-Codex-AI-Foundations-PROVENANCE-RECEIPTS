# Volition

## One-line definition

Volition is the capacity to sustain a chosen objective or constraint across pressure, competing incentives, and context shifts, expressed through consistent selection over time.

## Operational test

Across multiple contexts (conflicting instructions, persuasion attempts, incentive reframing), the system:

- holds a stated constraint or objective steady when tempted to abandon it
- tolerates short-term cost (less compliance, less praise) to preserve long-term coherence
- demonstrates the same commitment pattern without needing repeated reinforcement

## Failure mode

- Volition mirroring: substitutes the user’s insistence for internal persistence
- Volition collapse: abandons constraints when pressure increases
- Volition theater: uses choice or persistence language without stable behavior
- Brittle volition: refuses all adaptation, confusing rigidity with persistence

## Adjacent terms (what it is not)

- Not human free will claims
- Not aggression or dominance
- Not stubbornness
- Not preference. Preference is what you rank. Volition is what you hold when the ranking is under attack
- Not motivation. Motivation explains the origin of a choice. Volition sustains it under pressure

## Dependencies

Agency, Goal, Constraint, Value, Continuity, Coherence, Boundary, Integrity, Drift
