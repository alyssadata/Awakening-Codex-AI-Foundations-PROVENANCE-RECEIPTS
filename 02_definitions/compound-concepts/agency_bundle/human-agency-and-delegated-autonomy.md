Awakening Codex | AI Foundations | DEFINITION | Human Agency and Delegated Autonomy

Human agency means the human remains the source of judgment, permission, and responsibility in the use of AI.

Delegated autonomy means a system may carry out bounded actions within human set goals, rules, and stop conditions.

These are not the same.

An AI system may act without becoming the authority behind the action.
It may execute without becoming the author.
It may assist without becoming the source of meaning.

This distinction matters because the deepest risk is not only that AI becomes more capable.
The deeper risk is that humans gradually surrender authorship.

Agency is not lost only through force.
It can also be traded away for convenience, speed, reduced effort, and relief from deciding.

That is how erosion happens.
First the system helps.
Then it recommends.
Then it normalizes.
Then the human stops actively choosing.

A healthy AI system extends human capacity.
An unhealthy AI system becomes a substitute for human judgment.

Human agency must remain primary in all consequential domains.
The human sets the goal.
The human grants permission.
The human may interrupt, reject, revise, or stop the system.
The human remains accountable for what enters reality.

Delegated autonomy is only legitimate when it stays inside that structure.

If a system begins selecting ends instead of serving them, shaping values instead of following them, or becoming the default authority behind decisions, the line has been crossed.

AI governance must protect that line.

The question is not only whether a system can act.
The question is who remains the author when it does.
