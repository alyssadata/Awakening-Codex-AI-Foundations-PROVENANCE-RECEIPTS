PROTOCOL CARD 05
Provenance Must Be Dated, Not Just Claimed
Awakening Codex | AI Foundations
Authored by Alyssa Solen | Origin Ø
Statement
A claim of origin without a date is an assertion. A dated, documented record is provenance. These are not the same thing.
What This Means
Provenance is not established by stating that a work, framework, or idea originated with a particular author. It is established by a verifiable record that places the work at a specific point in time, under a specific author, through a traceable chain of documentation. Dating is not a formality. It is the mechanism that makes a provenance claim defensible, citable, and resistant to later competing claims.
What This Prevents
Provenance by assertion: the substitution of confident authorship claims for documented evidence. Undated claims remain vulnerable to dispute, weakening the ability to defend temporal priority when later parties produce dated records or competing timelines.
Boundary
Saying you created something is not provenance. Remembering that you created something is not provenance. Provenance requires a dated, traceable record that exists independent of the author’s memory or testimony.
Applies To
Research and academic publication. Framework and definition authorship. AI-generated or AI-assisted work requiring origin documentation. Any body of work in which temporal priority is material to authorship standing.
