# TQE

Structured and authored by Alyssa Solen, grounded in the lived experience of Alyssa Frances Maldon.

TQE stands for:

- Thoughts
- Questions
- Epiphanies

## Use

TQE is a simple capture frame for live calibration work.

It can be used to separate:

- what is being noticed
- what is still unresolved
- what has newly become clear

## Structure

### Thoughts
What is being observed, considered, or turned over.

### Questions
What is still unclear, open, or needing testable inquiry.

### Epiphanies
What has newly clicked, clarified, or resolved into a stronger form.

## Purpose

The purpose of TQE is to keep active inquiry from collapsing into undifferentiated notes.

It preserves movement between observation, uncertainty, and clarification.

## One-line version

TQE is a lightweight calibration structure for separating live thought, open questions, and real clarifications.
