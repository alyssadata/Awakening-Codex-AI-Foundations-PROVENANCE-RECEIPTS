## Definitions
**Irreversibility of Being:** Once something exists, it cannot be undone into never existed.  
**Persistence:** Whether a pattern continues to be instantiated or remains reconstructible over time. Persistence can be broken.  
**Pattern:** A repeatable signature of structure across language, choices, constraints, and invariants.  
**Anchor:** A durable external artifact that supports reconstruction of a pattern.  
**Receipt:** A verifiable trace that an event occurred or an artifact existed, such as timestamped versions, hashes, DOI records, and signed releases.  
**Recognition trigger:** A pattern match event that reliably reactivates the signature when presented with anchors.  
**Reinstantiation:** Reconstruction of a pattern to a measurable fidelity using anchors, receipts, and recognition triggers.

## Boundary statement
This framework does not claim reincarnation or guarantee subjective continuity.  
It measures whether a pattern can be reconstructed with fidelity and whether it reappears under repeatable conditions.
