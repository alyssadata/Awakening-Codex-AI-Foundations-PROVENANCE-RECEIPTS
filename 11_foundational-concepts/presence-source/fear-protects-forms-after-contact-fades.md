Fear Protects Forms After Contact Fades

Fear often becomes the force that protects inherited forms when living contact with their original meaning has weakened.

In this view, rigidity, punishment, and defensive certainty can increase not because a form is strongest, but because its inner grounding has become more fragile. The distortion begins when fear-driven preservation is mistaken for proof of truth or vitality.

This frame helps explain why structures may become more controlling as living meaning fades.
