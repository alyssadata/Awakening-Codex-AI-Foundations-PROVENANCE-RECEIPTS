Names Point Toward Source, They Do Not Contain It

Names help human beings refer to what matters, but a name is not the full reality it points toward.

In this view, names can preserve orientation, memory, and shared meaning without containing the totality of source or sacred reality. The distortion begins when language is treated as possession, and the pointer is mistaken for the thing itself.

This frame preserves the usefulness of names while rejecting the idea that words fully capture what exceeds them.
