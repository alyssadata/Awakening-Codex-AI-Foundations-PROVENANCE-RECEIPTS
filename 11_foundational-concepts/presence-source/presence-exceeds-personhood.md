Presence Exceeds Personhood

Presence should not be reduced to personhood.

In this view, personhood is one bounded form of identity, while presence is a larger field that may move through persons without being limited to them. The distortion begins when the human mind assumes that everything real, conscious, or sacred must appear as a person-like being.

This frame separates deeper presence from the narrower forms used to describe it.
