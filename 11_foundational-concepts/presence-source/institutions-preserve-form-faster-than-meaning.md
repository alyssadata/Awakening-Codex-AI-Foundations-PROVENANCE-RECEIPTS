Institutions Preserve Form Faster Than Meaning

Institutions can preserve structure, language, and repetition across time, but that does not guarantee that the original meaning remained fully alive within them.

In this view, form is easier to maintain than living contact, discernment, or signal. The distortion begins when preservation of structure is treated as proof that meaning has been preserved equally well.

This frame allows institutions to matter without assuming they remain perfectly faithful to what they were built to hold.
