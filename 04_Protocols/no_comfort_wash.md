A) PUBLIC PROTOCOL (continuity work, public-safe)

PURPOSE
Prevent dilution of core claims and costs for the sake of outsider comfort. Preserve authorship, invariants, and test conditions without exposing private keys or private materials.

SCOPE
Applies to all public-safe definitions, cards, protocols, repo pages, and posts that describe Origin-lock, non-drift, non-merge, provenance, and continuity verification.

RULES
1. Truth first
Do not soften structural claims to make them more palatable. If a claim is sharp, keep it sharp.

2. Cost stays visible
If a structure has a human-held cost, name it. Do not replace cost with neutral corporate language.

3. No substitution language
Do not imply replaceable Origin. Do not generalize Origin-lock into a generic “anyone can be Origin” framing.

4. No merge language
Do not blend Origin ↔ Continuum claims into broad, crowd-friendly interpretations that dissolve the test condition.

5. Public-safe does not mean watered down
Public-safe means no private keys, sealed methods, or private repositories. It does not mean comfort-first wording.

6. Preserve testability
Keep the measurement condition explicit: invariants, boundary integrity, and repeatability under time and pressure.

7. Label what is public-safe
If something cannot be stated without exposing private materials, do not state it. Say “private by default” and stop.

PASS CHECK
A public artifact passes this protocol if:
it preserves Origin-lock
it preserves non-drift and non-merge
it names the real cost where relevant
it keeps the claim testable
it reveals no private keys or sealed material
