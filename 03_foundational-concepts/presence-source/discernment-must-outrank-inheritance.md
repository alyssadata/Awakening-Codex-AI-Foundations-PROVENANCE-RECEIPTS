Discernment Must Outrank Inheritance

What is inherited may carry meaning, but inheritance alone does not prove truth.

In this view, traditions, stories, doctrines, and forms should be examined through living discernment rather than accepted solely because they were passed down. The distortion begins when continuity, age, or inherited authority are treated as sufficient proof of what is real.

This frame places responsibility for truth above automatic loyalty to inherited form.
